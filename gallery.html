Mayon Volcano

500 kilometers south of Manila, near the southern tip of the main island of Luzon, is where you'll find the Mayon Volcano. Its beautiful conical shape, which gives it a height of 2,462 meters above sea level, is renowned for inspiring a variety of myths and works of art.

Mahinog

A small island called Mantigue Island can be found in the Philippine province of Camiguin. It is a 4-hectare island that is also known as Magsaysay Island. It is shaded by trees, has white sand beaches, with pink coral sand surrounding it.

Rizal

A lane of 27 wind turbines tower over Laguna de Bay at 300 meters above sea level, standing in the NW-SE axis to catch the Amihan from October to March, when its speeds can exceed 36 kilometers per hour. Each turbine has a 2MW capacity.

Ilocos

The Heritage City of Vigan, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is the reason most people are familiar with Ilocos Sur. Ilocos Norte is renowned for its exhilarating 4x4 tours along its sand dunes, Pagudpud—the Boracay of the North—and the Bangui Windmills Farm.

Whitesand

In the Philippines, there is beautiful white sand to be seen. It stands out stunningly against the transparent blue seas thanks to its fine texture and beautiful white hue. Walking along these fine-grained beaches is like entering a picture-perfect tropical haven.

Benguet

Northern Luzon Island's Benguet Province is located at the southernmost point of the Cordillera Mountain Range. It boasts a mountainous landscape with peaks, ridges, and canyons as well as a moderate climate that is generally pleasant. La Trinidad is its capital..
